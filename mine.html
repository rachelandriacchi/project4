My Experiences & How To Help

Twice a year, we have a thing called SLCs at Da Vinci.
SLCs stand for Student Led Conferences. On those days, we have a shortened schedule.
The shortened schedule is:

Office Hours~ 7:50-8:45
Period 1~ 8:50-10:10
Period 2~ 10:15-11:30
Lunch~ 11:30-12:00
Period 3~ 12:05-1:20

Normal class periods are about 1 hour and 35 or 45 Minutes long.
During SLC weeks, class periods are 1 hour and 20 minutes long, meaning we only lost 15-25 minutes of class each day.
From experience, I saw that we were able to get the same amount of work done on shorter days as we did on regular days.
I had a worksheet to do for math one of the days and was still able to get the entire thing done in one period.
The only part of the schedule that would need to change is to make some time for seminars and advisory.
If an hour and 30 minutes were added, we could have about one hour for seminar and around 30 minutes for advisory.
